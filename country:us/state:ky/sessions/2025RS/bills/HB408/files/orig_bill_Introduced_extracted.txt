4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 As used in Sections 1 to 10 of this Act:

9 condition;

11 patient's attending physician, consulting physician, psychiatrist, or psychologist,
12 a patient has the ability to make and communicate an informed decision to health
13 care providers, including communication through persons familiar with the
14 patient's manner of communicating if those persons are available;
15 (4) "Consulting physician" means a physician licensed in Kentucky who is qualified
16 by specialty or experience to make a professional diagnosis and prognosis
17 regarding a patient's terminal condition;
18 (5) "Counseling" means one (1) or more consultations between a state-licensed
19 psychiatrist, psychologist, clinical social worker, or clinical professional
20 counselor and a patient for the purpose of determining that the patient is
21 competent and not suffering from a psychiatric or psychological disorder or
22 depression causing impaired judgment;
23 (6) "Health care facility" means a hospital, nursing facility, nursing home, or
24 hospice, public or private, whether organized for profit or not, that is licensed
25 pursuant to KRS Chapter 216B;
26 (7) "Health care provider" means a health care facility or a health care provider
27 licensed under KRS Chapter 311, 315, or 319;

14 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 311 IS CREATED TO
15 READ AS FOLLOWS:
16 (1) A patient has a right to information regarding all treatment options reasonably
17 available for the care of the patient, including but not limited to information in
18 response to specific questions about the foreseeable risks and benefits of
19 medication, without a physician's withholding requested information regardless
20 of the purpose of the questions or the nature of the information.
21 (2) A patient who is competent, is a resident of Kentucky, has been determined by an
22 attending physician and a consulting physician to be suffering from a terminal
23 condition, and has voluntarily expressed the wish to die may make a written
24 request for medication that the patient may self-administer in accordance with
25 Sections 1 to 10 of this Act. A patient shall not qualify under Sections 1 to 10 of
26 this Act solely because of age or disability.
27 (3) A valid written request for medication under Sections 1 to 10 of this Act shall be

4 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 311 IS CREATED TO
5 READ AS FOLLOWS:
6 (1) Not withstanding any state law to the contrary, an attending physician may
7 provide medication to a qualified patient to end the qualified patient's life upon a
8 request made by the qualified patient under Sections 1 to 10 of this Act.

10 (a) Make the initial determination of whether a patient has a terminal

23 terminal condition-directed treatment options;

25 diagnosis and for a determination that the patient is competent and acting
26 voluntarily;

8 SECTION 7. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 311 IS CREATED TO

12 health care provider is unable or unwilling to carry out the qualified patient's

14 any relevant medical records for the patient to a new health care provider upon

17 from participating, or entering into an agreement to participate, in activities
18 under Sections 1 to 10 of this Act while on premises owned, under

20 acting within the course and scope of any employment by, or contract with,
21 the health care provider.
22 (b) A health care provider that adopts a policy described in paragraph (a) of
23 this subsection shall give notice of the policy prohibiting participation to a
24 person or entity prior to that person or entity participating in activities
25 under Sections 1 to 10 of this Act. A health care provider that fails to
26 provide notice to a person or entity shall not enforce the policy against a
27 person or entity.

12 SECTION 8. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 311 IS CREATED TO
13 READ AS FOLLOWS:
14 (1) Nothing in Sections 1 to 10 of this Act shall authorize a physician or any other
15 person to end a patient's life by lethal injection, mercy killing, or active
16 euthanasia.
17 (2) Actions taken by any person or entity in participating in activities including
18 acting as a witness or interpreter under Sections 1 to 10 of this Act shall not, for
19 any purpose, constitute suicide, assisted suicide, mercy killing, or homicide under
20 state law.
21 (3) The cause of death recorded on a certificate of death under KRS 213.076 for the
22 death of a qualified patient under Sections 1 to 10 of this Act shall be related to
23 the terminal condition of the qualifying patient and shall not be recorded as
24 suicide, assisted suicide, mercy killing, or homicide under state law.
