2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 259.120 is amended to read as follows:
4 Stray equines and stray cattle shall be taken up and posted in the following manner:
5 (1) (a) Documentation of stray equines shall be taken before a county
6 judge/executive of the district, who shall administer to the taker-up an oath, in
7 substance, that the equine was taken up by him as a stray and that he has not
8 defaced or altered the marks, brands, or other identifiers, including but not
9 limited to microchips or freeze brands, of the equine.
10 (b) Documentation of stray cattle shall be taken before a county judge/executive
11 of the district, who shall administer to the taker-up an oath, in substance that
12 the cattle were taken by him as strays on his premises within the preceding ten
13 (10) days and that he has not defaced or altered the marks or brands of the
14 cattle.
15 (c) Duties of the county judge/executive pertaining to stray equines shall be to:
16 1. Contract with a licensed veterinarian, who shall document the stray
17 equine's breed, color, sex, marks, brands, scars, and other distinguishing
18 features, perform a microchip scan, and identify the existence of lip
19 tattoos, freeze brands, or microchips;
20 2. Record the veterinarian's findings, the name and residence of the taker-
21 up, and the location of the stray equine in a book to be kept by him for
22 that purpose;
23 3. Maintain documentation in electronic and paper format; and
24 4. Send a copy of the documentation of the stray equine to the Office of
25 [the ]State Veterinarian, who shall post notification on the Office of [the
26 ]State Veterinarian's website[Web site]. The Office of[ the] State
27 Veterinarian shall post one (1) photograph of the stray equine's front

1 view, including its head and feet, and one (1) photograph of the stray

3 (d) The county judge/executive:
4 1. May contract, subject to approval by the county fiscal court, with
5 a person or organization that provides animal sheltering services
6 to perform those duties listed under this subsection and
7 subsection (2) of this section in lieu of the county
8 judge/executive; and
9 2. Shall be immune from criminal and civil liability under KRS
10 259.140 for any act related to the taking up and posting of stray
11 equines;
12 (2) The county judge/executive shall give to the taker-up a copy of the documentation
13 for the record and immediately deliver to the county clerk a certified copy of the
14 same record;
15 (3) The clerk shall immediately record the stray certificate of the county
16 judge/executive as provided by the taker-up in a book to be kept by him for that
17 purpose;
18 (4) The taker-up shall immediately post a copy of the county judge/executive's
19 certificate in the sheriff's office with jurisdiction over the area where the stray cattle
20 or stray equine was taken up after he has posted the stray. Hold time for stray
21 equines shall begin after all documentation has been properly filed and posted by
22 the county judge/executive and taker-up; and
23 (5) (a) If ownership is found from identifiers of the stray equine such as lip tattoos,
24 freeze brands, or microchips, efforts shall be made by the county/judge
25 executive or his designee to ascertain the owner by investigatory due
26 diligence in locating the owner and providing notice before holding time
27 expires. The owner/claimants of the stray equine shall reimburse the county

1 judge/executive for the cost of the veterinarian's assessment per the contracted
2 agreement.
3 (b) The taker-up shall be paid by the owner of the stray, if and when he claims the
4 stray or its value, the actual itemized costs incurred by the taker-up for
5 keeping the stray equine or cattle. In the event that a dispute arises relating to
6 ownership, adverse claimants, third-party claims or liens, value of the equine,
7 or actual itemized expenses incurred, the parties may file an action in a court
8 of competent jurisdiction of the county in which the stray equine was taken
9 up. The filing of an action under this paragraph shall toll holding time as to
10 vesting of ownership interests.
11 (c) The taker-up may have the stray equine sterilized only after the fifteen (15)
12 day holding period has expired and ownership vested pursuant to KRS
13 259.130, and any pending court cases pertaining to the stray equine have been
