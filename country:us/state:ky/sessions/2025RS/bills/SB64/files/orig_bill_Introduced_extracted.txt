UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 1112
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 511.100 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) As used in this section:
5 (a) "Key infrastructure assets" means:
6 1. Any critical node of a system used in the production or generation of
7 electrical energy;
8 2. A petroleum refinery;
9 3. A rubber or hazardous chemical manufacturing facility;
10 4. A petroleum or hazardous chemical storage facility or terminal;
11 5. Natural gas processing, fractionation, stabilization, and compressor
12 station facilities, as well as above-ground natural gas or petroleum
13 pipelines and related facilities;
14 6. Railroad yards and railroad tunnel portals;
15 7. A drinking water collection, treatment, or storage facility;
16 8. Grounds or property of a state prison, juvenile justice facility, jail, or
17 other facility for the detention of persons charged with or convicted of
18 crimes;
19 9. A facility used for research, development, design, production, delivery,
20 or maintenance of military weapons systems, subsystems, and
21 components or parts to meet military requirements of the United States;
22 10. A wireless communications facility, including the tower, antennae,
23 support structures and all associated ground-based equipment, and a
24 telecommunications central switching office;
25 11. A facility, equipment, or communication line used in the delivery of
26 cable television , telephony, or broadband service[headend];
27 12. A commercial food manufacturing or processing facility in which food

1 is manufactured, processed, or packaged, commercially, for human
2 consumption, but not including retail food establishments, home-based
3 processors, or home-based microprocessors as those terms are defined in
4 KRS 217.015;
5 13. An animal feeding operation as defined in 40 C.F.R. sec. 122.23; or
6 14. A concentrated animal feeding operation as defined in 40 C.F.R. sec.
7 122.23; and
8 (b) "Unmanned aircraft system" means an aircraft that is operated without the
9 possibility of direct human interaction from within or on the aircraft and
10 includes everything that is on board or otherwise attached to the aircraft and
11 all associated elements, including communication links and the components
12 that control the small unmanned aircraft, that are required for the safe and
13 efficient operation of the unmanned aircraft in the national airspace system.
14 (2) (a) A person commits the offense of trespass upon key infrastructure assets if he
15 or she knowingly enters or remains unlawfully in or upon real property on
16 which key infrastructure assets are located.
17 (b) A person commits the offense of trespass upon key infrastructure assets if he
18 or she knowingly uses, or retains or authorizes a person to use, an unmanned
19 aircraft system to fly above real property on which key infrastructure assets
20 are located with the intent to cause harm or damage to or conduct surveillance
21 of the key infrastructure asset without the prior consent of the owner, tenant,
22 or lessee of the real property.
23 (c) A person commits the offense of trespass upon key infrastructure assets if he
24 or she knowingly, without consent of the owner or authorized representative:
25 1. Operates an unmanned aircraft system, video recording device, audio
26 recording device, or photography equipment on or above property
27 containing a key infrastructure asset referenced in subsection (1)(a)12.,

2 2. Records or distributes, photographically, electronically, or otherwise,
3 any part, procedure, or action of a key infrastructure asset referenced in
4 subsection (1)(a)12., 13., or 14. of this section.
5 (3) Trespass upon key infrastructure assets is a Class B misdemeanor for the first
6 offense, and a Class A misdemeanor for a second or subsequent offense.
7 (4) This section does not apply to:
8 (a) An unmanned aircraft system used by the federal government or by the
9 Commonwealth, or by a person acting pursuant to a contract with the federal
10 government or the Commonwealth;
11 (b) An unmanned aircraft system used by:
12 1. The owner of the real property or key infrastructure asset;
13 2. A person under a valid lease, servitude, right-of-way, right of use,
14 permit, license, or other right granted by the owner of the real property
15 or key infrastructure asset; or
16 3. A third party who is retained or authorized by a person specified in
17 subparagraph 1. or 2. of this paragraph;
18 (c) An unmanned aircraft system used by a law enforcement agency, emergency
19 medical service agency, hazardous material response team, disaster
20 management agency, or other emergency management agency for the purpose
21 of incident command, area reconnaissance, personnel and equipment
22 deployment monitoring, training, or a related purpose;
23 (d) Operation of an unmanned aircraft system by a person or entity for a
24 commercial purpose in compliance with applicable Federal Aviation
25 Administration authorization, regulations, or exemptions;
26 (e) A satellite orbiting the earth;
27 (f) An unmanned aircraft system used by an insurance company or a person

3 (g) An unmanned aircraft system used strictly in accordance with an order of a

6 behalf of any electric, water, or natural gas utility company for legitimate
7 business purposes; or
8 (i) Any federal, state, or local government law enforcement or regulatory officer
9 or employee while the officer or employee is engaged in the performance of
10 his or her official duties.
11 Section 2. KRS 512.020 is amended to read as follows:
12 (1) A person is guilty of criminal mischief in the first degree when, having no right to
13 do so or any reasonable ground to believe that he or she has such right, he or she
14 intentionally or wantonly:
15 (a) Defaces, destroys, or damages any property causing pecuniary loss of five
16 hundred dollars ($500) or more;
17 (b) Damages, possesses, or tampers with the operations of a key infrastructure
18 asset, as defined in KRS 511.100, in a manner that renders the asset
19 inoperable, in whole or in part, or renders the operation of the
20 asset[operations] harmful or dangerous; or
21 (c) As a tenant, intentionally or wantonly defaces, destroys, or damages
22 residential rental property causing pecuniary loss of five hundred dollars
23 ($500) or more.
24 (2) Criminal mischief in the first degree is a Class D felony, unless:
25 (a) The offense occurs during a declared emergency as defined by KRS 39A.020
26 arising from a natural or man-made disaster, within the area covered by the
27 emergency declaration, and within the area impacted by the disaster, in which

1 case it is a Class C felony;
2 (b) For the first offense, if the defendant at any time prior to trial effects repair or
3 replacement of the defaced, destroyed, or damaged property, makes complete
4 restitution in the amount of the damage, or performs community service as
5 required by the court, in which case it is a Class B misdemeanor. The court
6 shall determine the number of hours of community service commensurate
7 with the total amount of monetary damage caused by or incidental to the
8 commission of the crime, of not less than sixty (60) hours; or
9 (c) For the second or subsequent offense, if the defendant at any time prior to trial
10 effects repair or replacement of the defaced, destroyed, or damaged property,
11 makes complete restitution in the amount of the damage, or performs
12 community service as required by the court, in which case it is a Class A
13 misdemeanor. The court shall determine the number of hours of community
14 service commensurate with the total amount of monetary damage caused by
15 or incidental to the commission of the crime, of not less than sixty (60) hours.
