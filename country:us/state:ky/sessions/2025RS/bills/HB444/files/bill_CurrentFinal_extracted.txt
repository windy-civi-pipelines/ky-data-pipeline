UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS HB 444/GA
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 281A.170 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) The commercial driver's license shall be marked "commercial driver's license" and
5 "CDL" and shall be, to the maximum extent practicable, tamper proof. It shall
6 include but is not limited to the following information:
7 (a) The name and present resident address of the licensee;
8 (b) The licensee's photograph;
9 (c) A physical description of the licensee including sex, height, weight, and eye
10 color;
11 (d) The licensee's date of birth;
12 (e) The licensee's signature;
13 (f) The class or type of commercial motor vehicle or vehicles that the person is
14 authorized to drive together with any endorsements or restrictions;
15 (g) The name of this state;
16 (h) The dates between which the license is valid; and
17 (i) Any other information required by the cabinet, except for a person's Social
18 Security number.
19 (2) A commercial driver's license issued under this chapter shall contain a denotation
20 that either:
21 (a) The commercial driver's license is a voluntary travel ID identity document
22 that complies with the security standards set forth by Pub. L. No. 109-13,
23 Title II, and may be used for identification for federal purposes; or
24 (b) The commercial driver's license shall not be used for federal identification
25 purposes.
26 (3) A commercial driver's license shall be issued with classifications, endorsements,
27 and restrictions. Vehicles that require an endorsement shall not be driven unless the

1 proper endorsement appears on the license and the applicant has passed the
2 knowledge and skills test required by the State Police.
3 (a) Classifications:
4 1. Class A - Any combination of vehicles with a gross vehicle weight
5 rating of twenty-six thousand and one (26,001) pounds or more, if the
6 gross vehicle weight rating of the vehicle being towed is in excess of ten
7 thousand (10,000) pounds. Licensees with an "A" classification may
8 with the proper endorsement drive Class B and C vehicles;
9 2. Class B - Any single vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of
10 twenty-six thousand and one (26,001) pounds or more, and any vehicle
11 towing a vehicle not in excess of ten thousand (10,000) pounds gross
12 vehicle weight rating. Licensees with a "B" classification may with the
13 proper endorsements drive Class C vehicles;
14 3. Class C - Any single vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of less
15 than twenty-six thousand and one (26,001) pounds or any vehicle
16 towing a vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating not in excess of ten
17 thousand (10,000) pounds which includes:
18 a. Vehicles designed to transport sixteen (16) or more passengers,
19 including the driver; or
20 b. Vehicles used in the transportation of hazardous materials which
21 requires the vehicle to be placarded under 49 C.F.R. secs. 172.500
22 to 172.560, as adopted by administrative regulations of the
23 cabinet, pursuant to KRS Chapter 13A;
24 4. Class D - All other vehicles not listed in any other class, including
25 mopeds; and
26 5. Class M - Motorcycles. Licensees with a "M" classification may also
27 drive mopeds.

1 (b) Endorsements:
2 1. "H" - Authorizes the driver to operate a vehicle transporting hazardous
3 materials;
4 2. "T" - Authorizes operation of double trailers and triple trailers in those
5 jurisdictions allowing the operation of triple trailers;
6 3. "P" - Authorizes operation of vehicles carrying passengers;
7 4. "N" - Authorizes operation of tank vehicles;
8 5. "X" - Authorizes operation of combination of hazardous materials and
9 tank vehicle endorsements;
10 6. "R" - Authorizes operation of all other endorsements not otherwise
11 specified; and

13 (c) The Transportation Cabinet shall promulgate administrative regulations in
14 accordance with KRS Chapter 13A to outline restrictions on the operation of

21 expire in eight (8) years unless the license was issued to a resident under the age of
22 twenty-one (21).

24 (a) Shall not be:
25 1. Licensed to operate a Class A, B, or C vehicle unless he or she has a "K"

2 to transport hazardous materials in intrastate commerce.
3 (7) A commercial driver with a "K" restriction shall not drive a commercial motor
4 vehicle in interstate commerce, unless he or she is exempt pursuant to 49 C.F.R.

11 Section 2. KRS 281A.185 is amended to read as follows:
12 (1) The Commonwealth shall not mask, defer imposition of judgment, or allow an
13 individual to enter into a diversion program that would prevent a CLP or CDL
14 holder's conviction for any violation, in any type of motor vehicle, of a state or local
15 traffic control law from appearing on the CDLIS driver record, whether the driver's
16 conviction was[driver was convicted] for an offense committed in the
17 Commonwealth or another state.
18 (2) This section shall not apply to the following violations:
19 (a) Parking;
20 (b) Vehicle weight; or
21 (c) Vehicle defect.
22 (3) When any actions under subsection (1) of this section occur, the conviction must
23 be reported from the court to the licensing agency to be recorded on the driver's
24 record and trigger any appropriate disqualifying action.
