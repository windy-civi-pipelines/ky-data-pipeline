UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 1970
2 Restoration of Voting Rights Task Force.
3 WHEREAS, the right to vote is a foundational principle of American democracy,
4 reinforcing civic responsibility and engagement; and
5 WHEREAS, Section 145 of the Constitution of Kentucky excludes from the right of
6 suffrage individuals convicted of a felony, ensuring accountability and upholding the rule
7 of law; and
8 WHEREAS, many Kentuckians who have completed their sentences have
9 demonstrated a commitment to reentering society as law-abiding and productive citizens;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, the current process for restoring voting rights is often complex and
12 inefficient, creating unnecessary obstacles for individuals seeking to fully reintegrate into
13 their communities; and
14 WHEREAS, promoting responsible civic participation through an efficient, fair,
15 and secure voting rights restoration process strengthens public trust in government and
16 the electoral system; and
17 WHEREAS, there is a need to ensure that any changes to voting rights restoration
18 are carefully considered, maintaining a balance between accountability, fairness, and
19 election integrity; and
20 WHEREAS, Kentucky has an interest in reviewing and refining its approach to
21 voting rights restoration while upholding the security and credibility of its electoral
22 process;
23 NOW, THEREFORE,
24 Be it resolved by the Senate of the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of
25 Kentucky, the House of Representatives concurring therein:
26 Section 1. The Legislative Research Commission shall establish the Restoration
27 of Voting Rights Task Force. The task force shall review, study, and make

1 recommendations regarding the current language of the Constitution relating to the right
2 to vote, and possible constitutional amendments or statutory changes to restore voting
3 rights to individuals with felony convictions who have completed their sentences. The
4 task force shall review existing laws and proposed amendments regarding voting rights
5 restoration, seek input from impacted individuals and experts, and develop
6 recommendations for legislative or constitutional amendments to be considered by the
7 General Assembly.
8 Section 2. The task force shall be composed of the following members with
9 final membership of the task force being subject to the consideration and approval of the
10 Legislative Research Commission:
11 (1) Five members of the House of Representatives, four of whom shall be
12 appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives, with one serving as co-chair
13 of the task force, and one of whom shall be appointed by the Minority Floor Leader of the
14 House of Representatives;
15 (2) Five members of the Senate, four of whom shall be appointed by the President
16 of the Senate, with one serving as co-chair of the task force, and one of whom shall be
17 appointed by the Minority Floor Leader of the Senate; and
18 (3) Any additional members as deemed necessary by the task force to fulfill its
19 objectives.
20 Section 3. The task force shall meet at least monthly during the 2025 Interim of
21 the General Assembly unless otherwise directed by the commission, and shall be staffed
22 by the Legislative Research Commission.
23 Section 4. The task force shall submit a report of its findings and
24 recommendations to the Legislative Research Commission no later than December 1,
25 2025, for referral to the General Assembly and consideration in the 2026 Regular
26 Session.
