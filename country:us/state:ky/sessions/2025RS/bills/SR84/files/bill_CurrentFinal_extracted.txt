3 WHEREAS, Elizabeth Jean Tori departed this earthly life on October 2, 2024,
4 leaving behind the memory of a woman who was dearly loved and who will be deeply
5 missed by all who knew her; and
6 WHEREAS, Elizabeth Tori was a longtime resident of Radcliff, where she owned
7 the Tori Shoppe and Tori Enterprises, and was instrumental in starting the Radcliff
8 Women’s Club; and
9 WHEREAS, Elizabeth Tori attended the University of Kentucky and Western
10 Kentucky University; and
11 WHEREAS, Elizabeth Tori began her political activism with the creation of the
12 North Hardin Republican Women’s Club, and was also active in the congressional
13 campaigns of her late husband, Martin, who ran for Kentucky’s second district United
14 States House of Representatives seat in 1988 and 1990; and
15 WHEREAS, Elizabeth Tori was the first woman and first Republican to win
16 election in the 10th Senate District and the selflessly represented District 10 from 1995 to
17 2011; and
18 WHEREAS, Elizabeth Tori chaired the Senate Veterans, Military Affairs, and
19 Public Protection Committee from 2005 through 2010 and served as both the Senate
20 Minority and Majority Whip. But she let it be known that the proudest moments of her
21 Senate career involved the passage of veterans' legislation; and
22 WHEREAS, as Senate President Robert Stivers said of Elizabeth Tori, she was as
23 kind of a person as you would ever want to know, who had a fierce loyalty to Kentucky
24 and all of its residents. Elizabeth was passionate about those who served in the military
25 and advocated for our active-duty members and retirees who lived here. She was also
26 very dedicated to her husband, Martin, and the family they shared"; and
27 WHEREAS, House Speaker David Osborne said, "As a mother, grandmother, small
