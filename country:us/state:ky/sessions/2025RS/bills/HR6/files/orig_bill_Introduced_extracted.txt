UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 172
2 WHEREAS, anti-choking devices are portable suction devices used to remove an
3 airway obstruction during a choking emergency and registered as a Class II medical
4 device with the United States Food and Drug Administration; and
5 WHEREAS, according to the American Academy of Pediatrics, at least one child
6 dies from choking on food every five days in the United States, and more than 12,000
7 children are taken to a hospital emergency room each year for food-choking injuries; and
8 WHEREAS, in 2017, 184 children and adolescents, from birth to 17 years old, died
9 from nonfood object-related aspiration in the United States; and
10 WHEREAS, Landon McCubbins, an 8-year-old from Boston, Kentucky, died after
11 choking on a bouncy ball at school in 2022; and
12 WHEREAS, anti-choking devices, such as LifeVac, have been acquired by various
13 Kentucky schools and over 6,700 schools in the United States. LifeVac announced in
14 May 2024 that 52 lives have been saved in school settings as a result of the use of the
15 company's devices;
16 NOW, THEREFORE,
17 Be it resolved by the House of Representatives of the General Assembly of the
18 Commonwealth of Kentucky:
19 Section 1. The House of Representatives encourages private and public
20 Kentucky schools to acquire anti-choking devices.
21 Section 2. The House of Representatives extends its most profound sympathy
22 upon the passing of Landon McCubbins, and expresses heartfelt condolences to his
23 family in this time of loss.
24 Section 3. The Clerk of the House of Representatives is directed to transmit a
25 copy of this Resolution to the Kentucky Department of Education, and Representative
26 Candy Massaroni for delivery to the family of Landon McCubbins.
