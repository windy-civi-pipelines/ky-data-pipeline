3 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. was born on October 3, 1928, to Robert and Julia

5 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. was a member of the Mississippi House of

9 achievement that paved the way for greater representation and progress in the state’s
10 governance; and

13 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. championed education reform, economic
14 development, and civil rights, always advocating for the betterment of his constituents
15 and the people of Mississippi; and
16 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. served in the Mississippi legislature until 2003, a
17 tenure in public service that included serving as chair of the education committee and
18 later as speaker pro tempore, marking him as a pioneering leader who helped shape the
19 legislative landscape of Mississippi; and
20 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. leaves behind a lasting legacy of courage,
21 perseverance, and leadership that transcends state lines, inspiring all those who strive for
22 justice and equality; and
23 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. was laid to rest on March 11, 2025, in the presence
24 of family and friends; and
25 WHEREAS, Robert G. Clark Jr. was preceded in death by his first wife, Essie, in
26 1978. He is survived by his wife, Jo Ann; three children; and numerous relatives and
27 friends;
