2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 189.993 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) Any person who violates KRS 189.045 shall be fined not less than one hundred
5 dollars ($100) nor more than one thousand dollars ($1,000).
6 (2) Any person convicted of violating any of the provisions of KRS 189.095 shall be
7 fined sixty dollars ($60) and costs of prosecution.
8 (3) Any person who violates any provision of KRS 189.205 shall be fined not less than
9 twenty dollars ($20) nor more than one hundred dollars ($100).
10 (4) Any person who violates any provision of KRS 189.375 shall be fined not less than
11 twenty dollars ($20) nor more than one hundred dollars ($100).
12 (5) Any person who violates KRS 189.505 shall:
13 (a) For the first offense, be fined one thousand dollars ($1,000)[not less than
14 sixty dollars ($60) nor more than two hundred dollars ($200)] or be
15 imprisoned for not more than thirty (30) days, or both;
16 (b) For a second or subsequent offense, be fined two thousand dollars ($2,000)
17 or be imprisoned for not more than thirty (30) days, or both; and
18 (c) In accordance with KRS 70.155 or 82.625, have any motor vehicle used by
19 the person in the commission of the violation impounded for not less than
20 six (6) months.
21 (6) Any person found violating any provision of KRS 189.820 or 189.830 is guilty of a
22 misdemeanor and shall be fined not less than twenty dollars ($20) nor more than
23 thirty-five dollars ($35).
24 (7) Any person who violates KRS 189.920 shall be fined not less than one hundred
25 dollars ($100) nor more than one thousand dollars ($1,000), or imprisoned in the
26 county jail for not more than thirty (30) days, or both. In the case of a private
27 vehicle not authorized to use emergency lights under KRS 189.920, all lighting and

3 (8) Any person who violates KRS 189.930 shall be fined not less than sixty dollars
4 ($60) nor more than five hundred dollars ($500), or be imprisoned in the county jail
5 for not more than thirty (30) days, or both.
6 (9) Any person who violates KRS 189.940 shall be fined not less than sixty dollars
7 ($60) nor more than one thousand dollars ($1,000) or be imprisoned in the county
8 jail for not more than six (6) months, or both. In the case of a private vehicle, except
9 as outlined in subsection (11) of this section, all lighting and other equipment used
10 in violation of KRS 189.910 to 189.950 shall be confiscated and forfeited to the

19 conviction of a second offense he or she shall be permanently barred from
20 employment or membership in any fire department, ambulance service, rescue
21 squad, police department, or sheriff's office in the Commonwealth, nor shall he or
22 she be permitted to operate any public safety vehicle as defined in KRS 189.910.

24 ($100) for the first offense, two hundred dollars ($200) for the second offense,
25 and one thousand dollars ($1,000) for each subsequent offense.
26 (b) Except as provided in paragraph (a) of this subsection, any person who
27 violates KRS 189.950 shall be fined not less than one hundred dollars ($100)

2 jail for not more than thirty (30) days, or both. In the case of a privately
3 owned vehicle, all lighting and other equipment used or installed in violation
4 of KRS 189.910 to 189.950 shall be confiscated and forfeited to the county in
5 which the offense occurred.

7 otherwise provided shall, upon conviction, be fined not less than twenty dollars
8 ($20) nor more than one hundred dollars ($100) for each offense, except that no
9 penalty shall be assessed for a violation of KRS 189.580(1)(b) or (6)(b).

11 resources over the highways of the Commonwealth in excess of the weight limits
12 without possessing a resource recovery road hauling permit. Violation for hauling
13 in excess of prescribed limits without possession of a permit or transporting natural
14 resources over prescribed limits of the resource recovery road hauling permit shall
15 be not less than five hundred dollars ($500) nor more than one thousand dollars
16 ($1,000) for each violation and shall be deposited in the resource recovery road
17 fund.
