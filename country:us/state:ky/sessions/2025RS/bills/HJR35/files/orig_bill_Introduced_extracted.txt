3 WHEREAS, William Lynwood Montell was born February 18, 1931, the second
4 child of Willie Guy Montell and Hazel Chapman Montell, in Rock Bridge, a small
5 community in Monroe County, Kentucky, which he described as "God's country; and
6 WHEREAS, William Lynwood Montell attended Campbellsville College, the
7 University of Kentucky, and Western Kentucky University, where he became interested
8 in history and folk studies; and
9 WHEREAS, after teaching history at Temple High School, William Lynwood
10 Montell applied to a Ph.D. program in Folk Studies at Indiana University. He received a
11 three-year fellowship pursuing what became his life-long passion--the study and
12 recording of the everyday history of the common man; and
13 WHEREAS, after receiving his doctorate, Doctor William Lynwood Montell taught
14 history and folk studies at Campbellsville College, where he was appointed as the
15 academic dean and later served as vice president of the college; and
16 WHEREAS, in 1969, Doctor William Lynwood Montell accepted a teaching
17 position in history and folk studies at Western Kentucky University, where he taught for
18 over 30 years. During this time, he was a visiting professor at the University of
19 California Los Angeles in 1971 and at Notre Dame University a few years later; and
20 WHEREAS, Doctor William Lynwood Montell was appointed the head of the
21 Center for Intercultural Studies at Western Kentucky University; and
22 WHEREAS, Doctor William Lynwood Montell was a past president of the
23 American Folklife Society and was well known across the United States by those in this
24 field of study; and
25 WHEREAS, Doctor William Lynwood Montell loved the area of Upper
26 Cumberland and he published or edited 28 books addressing all aspects of its local
27 history and folkways; and

1 WHEREAS, Doctor William Lynwood Montell was a master storyteller and was an
2 in-demand statewide speaker for the Kentucky Humanities Council;
3 WHEREAS, Doctor William Lynwood Montell received several awards, including
4 his 2001 induction into the Kentucky Music Hall of Fame. In 2003, he received the
5 Governor's Arts Award in the Folk Heritage category. He was recognized by the
6 Kentucky Southern Gospel Association in 2020 for his contribution to the preservation of
7 that music genre; and
8 WHEREAS, Doctor William Lynwood Montell loved and celebrated the everyday
9 man, and it is fitting and proper that his contributions be memorialized in some tangible
10 way;
11 NOW, THEREFORE,
12 Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
13 Section 1. The Transportation Cabinet shall designate the Tompkinsville
14 Bypass, on Kentucky Route 163 in Monroe County, as the "Doctor William Lynwood
15 Montell Memorial Highway" and shall, within 30 days of the effective date of this
16 Resolution, erect appropriate signage denoting this designation.
