2 Representative Thomas Martin Jones.
3 WHEREAS, Thomas Martin Jones was born on June 22, 1947, in Salvisa,
4 Kentucky, to the late Ezra and Eunice Jones; and
5 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones was a beloved and devoted husband to Ann, his wife
6 and best friend of 55 years, a hero to his sons, Tom and Steve, and the grandest of
7 grandfathers to Jake, Drew, Grace, and Annabelle; and
8 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones was a graduate of Mercer County High School and
9 earned a bachelor's degree and law degree from the University of Kentucky; and
10 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones proudly defended his country as a member of the
11 United States Army from 1969 to 1971; and
12 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones was strong in his faith and love for Jesus Christ, and at
13 the time of his passing was a devoted and valued member and former deacon at
14 Lawrenceburg First Christian Church; and
15 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones was a member of the Kentucky General Assembly from
16 1981 to 1990, spending his 30s and early 40s representing the 55th House District, which
17 was made up of Anderson, Mercer, and Spencer Counties; and
18 WHEREAS, as a legislator, Thomas Jones championed funding for public
19 education and teacher salaries, and was a strong proponent of the Kentucky Education
20 Reform Act of 1990. His efforts also contributed to the creation of the Lawrenceburg
21 campus of the Bluegrass Community and Technical College; and
22 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones was proud of his role in supporting Kentucky
23 Educational Television and Kentucky State Parks, among dozens of other programs and
24 agencies; and
25 WHEREAS, Thomas Jones served as an attorney in Lawrenceburg for nearly five
26 decades, providing support and counsel to countless clients and their loved ones during
27 difficult, stressful times. He took pride in being a small-town lawyer who helped
