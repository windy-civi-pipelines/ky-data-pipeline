3 WHEREAS, the founders of the Constitution of the United States empowered state
4 legislatures to be guardians of liberty against future abuses of power by the federal
5 government; and
6 WHEREAS, the federal government has invaded the legitimate role of the states
7 through the manipulative process of federal mandates, most of which are unfunded to a
8 great extent; and
9 WHEREAS, it is the solemn duty of the states to protect the liberty of our people,
10 particularly for generations to come, by proposing amendments to the Constitution of the
11 United States through a convention of the states under Article V of the Constitution of the
12 United States for the purpose of restraining these and related abuses of power; and
13 WHEREAS, it is imperative that once an Article V convention is established that
14 this Commonwealth have a process and procedure to select and provide oversight over
15 commissioners to ensure that they are acting in the best interests of the Commonwealth
16 and its people;
17 NOW, THEREFORE,
18 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:

24 Act;

4 of the appointed commissioners, their commissions, and instructions;
5 (5) "Delegation" means the group of commissioners and interim commissioners
6 chosen by the General Assembly to attend any interstate convention; and
7 (6) "Interim commissioner" means a person selected by the advisory committee
8 pursuant to Section 5 of this Act to fill a vacancy in the delegation.
9 SECTION 2. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 6 IS CREATED TO
10 READ AS FOLLOWS:
11 (1) If an Article V convention is convened and this Commonwealth is a participant,
12 the General Assembly shall adopt a commissioning resolution naming five (5)
13 commissioners to represent this Commonwealth in the convention.
14 (2) The commissioning resolution shall include but not be limited to the following
15 components:
16 (a) A commissioner shall not vote for or otherwise promote any change to the
17 traditional convention rule of decision on the floor and in the committee of
18 the whole, including that each state has one (1) vote; and
19 (b) A commissioner shall not vote in favor of any proposed amendment that
20 would alter the text of the specific guarantees of individual liberty
21 established by the Constitution of the United States, including the original
22 Constitution, the Bill of Rights, and the Thirteenth, Fourteenth, Fifteenth,
23 Nineteenth, Twenty-Third, Twenty-Fourth, and Twenty-Sixth Amendments
24 to the Constitution of the United States.
25 (3) If a commissioning resolution is passed pursuant to an Article V convention of
26 the states to propose amendments to the Constitution of the United States, it shall
27 clearly state the scope of the commissioners' authority, which shall be limited by:

4 (4) The General Assembly may provide additional instructions at any time by
5 subsequent joint resolution, a copy of which the Clerk of the House of
6 Representatives shall provide to each commissioner and to the advisory
7 committee.
8 SECTION 3. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 6 IS CREATED TO
9 READ AS FOLLOWS:
10 (1) At the time of their appointment, and throughout their commissionable service,
11 each of the five (5) commissioners shall:
12 (a) Have been a United States citizen for not less than five (5) years;
13 (b) Be a legal resident of the Commonwealth of Kentucky for not less than five
14 (5) years;
15 (c) Be at least twenty-five (25) years old;
16 (d) Be an active registered voter in the Commonwealth of Kentucky;
17 (e) Not be registered or have been required to register as a federal lobbyist at
18 any time within the last five (5) years;
19 (f) Not currently be a federal employee or federal contractor, nor have been a
20 federal employee or federal contractor within the last ten (10) years, but
21 may be a member of the United States Armed Forces;
22 (g) Not have held a federal elected or appointed office at any time within the
23 last ten (10) years;
24 (h) Not have had any felony convictions for any crime, including crimes
25 involving moral turpitude, in any jurisdiction within the last ten (10) years;
26 and
27 (i) Not hold a statewide office while performing the duties of commissioner or

1 interim commissioner. For the purposes of this section, a position as a state
2 legislator shall not be deemed a statewide office.
3 (2) (a) Each commissioner shall, before exercising any function of the position,
4 execute the following oath in writing: "I do solemnly swear or affirm that I
5 accept and will act according to the limits of authority specified in my
6 commission and any present or subsequent instructions from the Kentucky
7 General Assembly. I understand that violating this oath may subject me to
8 penalties provided by law. I understand that I may be recalled or suspended
9 from my duties by the General Assembly or the advisory committee."
10 (b) A commissioner's executed oath shall be filed with the Secretary of State.
11 (3) After a commissioner's executed oath is filed with the Secretary of State, the
12 Clerk of the House of Representatives shall provide to the commissioner an
13 official copy of the executed oath and the commissioning resolution, which
14 together shall serve as the commissioner's credentials.
15 (4) (a) A commissioner or interim commissioner may be recalled or removed at any
16 time for any reason by a joint resolution of the General Assembly, or if the
17 General Assembly is not in session, may be recalled and suspended from his
18 or her duties by the advisory committee, pending a vote of the General
19 Assembly the next time it is in session and authorized to vote on the issue.
20 (b) A commissioner or interim commissioner shall be recalled or suspended by
21 the advisory committee pursuant to a determination under Section 5 of this
22 Act that he or she has exceeded the scope of his or her authority.
23 (5) Any commissioner vacancy shall be filled by the advisory committee's selection of
24 an interim commissioner until the General Assembly selects a permanent
25 replacement.
26 (6) A commissioner shall receive the same compensation and allowance for expenses
27 as a member of the House of Representatives of this Commonwealth.

1 Compensation for the commissioner shall be prorated for length of time served.
2 (7) A commissioner or an interim commissioner shall not accept, during his or her
3 time of service, any gift or benefit with a combined value of more than fifty
4 dollars ($50), other than from a member of his or her family and of the kind
5 customarily granted by a member of one's family. As used in this subsection, the
6 term "gift or benefit" shall be construed liberally to include current and future
7 loans, lodging, food, offer of prospective employment, and other actual and
8 prospective benefits. An employer's decision to continue paying a commissioner's
9 current salary shall not be construed as a gift.
10 SECTION 4. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 6 IS CREATED TO
11 READ AS FOLLOWS:
12 (1) (a) The commissioners within the delegation, including any interim
13 commissioners filling a vacancy, shall choose from among them a person
14 to:
15 1. Chair the delegation;
16 2. Cast the state's vote on the convention floor; and
17 3. Speak to the media on behalf of the delegation.
18 (b) If the delegation so decides, the same person may exercise any two (2) or all
19 three (3) functions listed in paragraph (a) of this subsection. The delegation
20 may, at any time, designate a different commissioner to perform any of the
21 functions listed in paragraph (a) of this subsection.
22 (2) Each commissioner shall take care to avoid communicating the impression to any
23 person outside the delegation that the delegation is divided on a question on
24 which the delegation has taken a formal position, including but not limited to
25 casting a vote.
26 (3) A commissioner who is not the commissioner designated to communicate with the
27 media on behalf of the delegation shall not communicate with the media about

1 convention business during the convention or during any temporary recess or
2 temporary adjournment.
3 (4) A commissioner who violates subsection (2) or (3) of this section may be
4 suspended or recalled by the advisory committee or the General Assembly.
5 (5) Subsections (2) and (3) of this section shall not be construed to prevent a
6 commissioner from presenting his or her opinions to the convention or debating a
7 matter at the convention on which his or her delegation has not formally taken a
8 position.
9 (6) (a) The quorum for a decision by the delegation shall be a majority present and
10 voting at the time the question is called. Decisions may include but not be
11 limited to the:
12 1. Designation of commissioners for particular duties; and
13 2. Determination of how the state's vote shall be cast.
14 (b) A decision shall not be made and a vote shall not be cast if less than a
15 majority of the delegation votes on the question.
16 (7) If a quorum is present, the decision of the delegation shall be that of the majority
17 of those present and voting at the time the question is called.
18 SECTION 5. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 6 IS CREATED TO
19 READ AS FOLLOWS:
20 (1) The advisory committee shall consist of the following members:
21 (a) A member of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate;
22 (b) A member of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the
23 House of Representatives; and
24 (c) A member of the General Assembly:
25 1. Nominated by both the Speaker of the House and the President of the
26 Senate; and
27 2. Approved by the majority of those voting in each chamber.

UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 1199
1 (2) The advisory committee shall select one (1) of its members as chair.
2 (3) A commissioner may request that the advisory committee advise him or her
3 whether a prospective action by a commissioner would violate the commissioning
4 resolution or any subsequent instructions.
5 (4) The advisory committee shall:
6 (a) Communicate to the commissioner requesting advice pursuant to subsection
7 (3) of this section by any appropriate means a determination within twenty-
8 four (24) hours of receiving the request; and
9 (b) Have the authority to hire staff and develop appropriate procedures and
10 mechanisms for monitoring the convention, its committees, and
11 subcommittees.
12 (5) If the advisory committee has reason to believe that a commissioner or interim
13 commissioner has exceeded the scope of his or her authority, the advisory
14 committee shall notify the Speaker of the House of Representatives and the
15 President of the Senate.
16 (6) Upon request for a determination by the Speaker of the House and the President
17 of the Senate whether a commissioner or interim commissioner has exceeded the
18 scope of his or her authority, the advisory committee shall issue a determination
19 whether the commissioner or interim commissioner exceeded his or her authority.
20 The determination shall be expeditiously made and immediately communicated to
21 the person requesting it.
22 (7) Upon a determination that a commissioner or interim commissioner has exceeded
23 the scope of his or her authority pursuant to this section, the advisory committee
24 shall notify the Speaker of the House and the President of the Senate for further
25 action pursuant to subsection (4) of Section 3 of this Act.
