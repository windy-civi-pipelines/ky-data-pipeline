Section 1:
UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 89
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 186.763 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) Subject to the limitations in subsection (2) of this section, a person may operate a
5 fully autonomous vehicle on the highways of this state without a human driver
6 provided that the automated driving system is engaged and the vehicle meets the
7 following conditions:
8 (a) If a failure of the automated driving system occurs that renders that system
9 unable to perform the entire dynamic driving task relevant to its intended
10 operational design domain, the fully autonomous vehicle will achieve a
11 minimal risk condition;
12 (b) The fully autonomous vehicle is capable of operating in compliance with the
13 applicable traffic and motor vehicle safety laws and regulations of this state
14 when reasonable to do so, unless an exemption has been granted by the
15 Transportation Cabinet; and
16 (c) When required by federal law, the vehicle bears the required manufacturer's
17 certification label indicating that at the time of its manufacture it has been
18 certified to be in compliance with all applicable federal motor vehicle safety
19 standards, including any exemptions granted by the National Highway Traffic
20 Safety Administration.
21 (2) From July 15, 2024, until July 31, 2026, a fully autonomous vehicle for which the
22 declared gross weight of the vehicle and any towed unit is more than sixty-two
23 thousand (62,000) pounds shall have a human driver, with the appropriate
24 credentials to operate the vehicle, present in the vehicle to monitor the performance
25 of the vehicle and intervene if necessary.
26 (3) Prior to operating a fully autonomous vehicle on the highways of this state without
27 a human driver, a person shall submit a law enforcement interaction plan to the

1 Transportation Cabinet and the Department of Kentucky State Police that describes:
2 (a) How to communicate with a fleet support specialist who is available during
3 the times the vehicle is in operation;
4 (b) How to safely remove the fully autonomous vehicle from the roadway and
5 steps to safely tow the vehicle;
6 (c) How to recognize whether the automated driving system is engaged on the
7 fully autonomous vehicle; and
8 (d) Any additional information the manufacturer or owner deems necessary
9 regarding hazardous conditions or public safety risks associated with the
10 operation of the fully autonomous vehicle.
11 (4) A school district shall not operate a fully autonomous vehicle as a school bus, as
12 defined in KRS 156.153, or use any fully autonomous vehicle to transport
13 students for any purpose.
