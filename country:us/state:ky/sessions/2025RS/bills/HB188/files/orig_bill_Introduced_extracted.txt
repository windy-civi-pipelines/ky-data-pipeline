Section 1:
UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 136
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 281 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) (a) A driveaway business that holds a proper driveaway certificate issued under
6 KRS 281.630 may apply for a driveaway plate with the county clerk in each
7 county in which his or her principal office or place of business, branch
8 office, sub-agent, or agency is located.
9 (b) A driveaway plate allows a vehicle to be operated in driveaway service and
10 shall be reusable until the driveaway plate expires or is revoked.
11 (c) The registration fee for initial issuance and annual renewal of a driveaway
12 plate is one hundred fifty dollars ($150) and is payable to the county clerk.
13 (2) Upon receipt of the one hundred fifty dollar ($150) fee, the county clerk shall
14 issue a certificate of registration and one (1) driveaway plate. The county clerk
15 shall furnish each driveaway business that holds a driveaway plate registered
16 under this section with additional driveaway plates upon the payment of a thirty-
17 five dollar ($35) fee for each additional plate requested. Fifteen dollars ($15) of
18 this fee shall be retained by the county clerk for each additional plate issued.
19 (3) A driveaway plate issued under this section shall:
20 (a) Not be used on a vehicle operated on a highway except for the purpose of
21 transporting vehicles in transit;
22 (b) Not be used by tow truck operators transporting wrecked, disabled,
23 abandoned, improperly parked, or burned vehicles; and
24 (c) Be used only by owners, corporate officers, agents, or employees of the
25 business to which the plate was issued.
26 (4) Holders of a driveaway plate shall keep a record of each trip made using a
27 driveaway plate. This record shall include the:

1 (a) Vehicle identification number of the vehicle;
2 (b) Origin and destination of the vehicle;
3 (c) Total number of miles traveled; and
4 (d) Dates traveled.
5 (5) Driveaway plates issued under this section shall annually expire on December 31.
6 (6) If a driveaway certificate is revoked or not renewed, all driveaway plates issued
7 under this section to the driveaway business holding that driveaway certificate
8 shall be returned to the cabinet.
9 (7) The cabinet shall be responsible for the issuance and cancellation of driveaway
10 plates under this section, and the Motor Vehicle Commission shall be responsible
11 for the enforcement of this section, except for the normal responsibilities of law
12 enforcement agencies. The cabinet may promulgate administrative regulations in
13 accordance with KRS Chapter 13A to administer this section.
14 Section 2. KRS 281.631 is amended to read as follows:
15 (1) No person shall act as a motor carrier without first obtaining a motor carrier vehicle
16 license from the department for each motor carrier vehicle.
17 (2) Application for and renewal of a motor carrier vehicle license shall be made in such
18 form as the department may require. Every motor carrier vehicle license shall be
19 renewed annually.
20 (3) (a) Except for driveaway plates issued under Section 1 of this Act, and except as
21 permitted under paragraph (b) of this subsection, an applicant or license
22 holder shall pay to the department the following annual license fees:
23 1. Thirty dollars ($30) for each taxicab, limousine, TNC, or disabled
24 persons vehicle;
25 2. Ten dollars ($10) for each motor carrier vehicle transporting household
26 goods for hire;
27 3. One hundred dollars ($100) for each charter bus or bus;
