2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 6.072 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) Witnesses testifying before[attending] the General Assembly,[ either house or] a[
5 legislative] committee, interim committee, statutory committee, subcommittee,
6 commission, or task force of the General Assembly, are entitled to the same
7 privileges and immunities, and the same compensation, as witnesses in other civil
8 and criminal proceedings[cases]. When summoned by the Commonwealth, they
9 shall be paid out of the State Treasury.
10 (2) Any member acting as chair[Chairmen] of a[the] committee,[committees] interim
11 committee, statutory committee, subcommittee, commission, or task force of the
12 General Assembly[Senate and House of Representatives] shall[may] administer
13 an[all necessary oaths] oath to any person who appears or is called to testify before
14 that[a] committee, interim committee, statutory committee, subcommittee,
15 commission, or task force of the General Assembly[ of either house].
16 (3) (a) The oath administered to each witness who testifies before a committee,
17 interim committee, statutory committee, subcommittee, commission, or task
18 force of the General Assembly shall follow the oath administered to a
19 witness in a court of law and read as follows: "Do you swear or affirm that
20 you will tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth?"
21 (b) If a witness refuses to take the oath, then he or she shall not be permitted to
22 testify before the committee.
23 (4) An oath sworn during a previous committee, interim committee, statutory
24 committee, subcommittee, commission, or task force meeting shall not apply to
25 any future meeting of a committee, interim committee, statutory committee,
26 subcommittee, commission, or task force of the General Assembly.
27 (5) Testimony given under oath shall be subject to the penalties prescribed in KRS
