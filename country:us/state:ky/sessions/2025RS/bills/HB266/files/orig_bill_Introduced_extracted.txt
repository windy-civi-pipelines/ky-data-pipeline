Section 1:
UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 1364
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF SUBCHAPTER 12 OF KRS CHAPTER
4 154 IS CREATED TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section:
6 (a) "In-demand industry sector" means an industry sector that has a
7 substantial current or potential impact on the state, regional, or local
8 economy, and that contributes to the growth or stability of other supporting
9 businesses or the growth of other industry sectors; and
10 (b) "In-demand occupation" means an occupation that currently has or is
11 projected to have a number of positions in an industry sector so as to have a
12 substantial impact on the state, regional, or local economy.
13 (2) By January 1 of each year, the cabinet shall:
14 (a) Create lists of in-demand industry sectors and in-demand occupations using
15 state and regional business and labor market projections, including the use
16 of labor market information; and
17 (b) Transmit the lists described in paragraph (a) of this subsection to the
18 Cabinet for Health and Family Services for use in determining eligibility
19 criteria for the Child Care Assistance Program as defined in Section 2 of
20 this Act.
21 (3) The cabinet shall promulgate administrative regulations in accordance with KRS
22 Chapter 13A to implement this section.
23 Section 2. KRS 199.894 is amended to read as follows:
24 As used in KRS 199.892 to 199.896, unless the context otherwise requires:
25 (1) "Cabinet" means the Cabinet for Health and Family Services;
26 (2) "Child Care and Development Fund" has the same meaning as in 45 C.F.R. sec.
27 98.2;

1 (3) "Child Care Assistance Program" means Kentucky's child-care subsidy program
2 providing families who meet state eligibility requirements with financial
3 resources to find and afford quality child care["Secretary" means secretary for
4 health and family services];
5 (4)[(3)] "Child-care center" means any child-care center that provides full- or part-
6 time care, day or night, to four (4) or more children in a nonresidential setting who
7 are not the children, grandchildren, nieces, nephews, or children in legal custody of
8 the operator. "Child-care center" shall not include any child-care facility operated
9 by a religious organization while religious services are being conducted, or a youth
10 development agency. For the purposes of this section, "youth development agency"
11 means a program with tax-exempt status under 26 U.S.C. sec. 501(c)(3), which
12 operates continuously throughout the year as an outside-school-hours center for
13 youth who are six (6) years of age or older, and for which there are no fee or
14 scheduled-care arrangements with the parent or guardian of the youth served;
15 (5)[(4)] "Department" means the Department for Community Based Services;[ and]
16 (6)[(5)] "Family child-care home" means a private home that is the primary residence
17 of an individual who provides full- or part-time care, day or night, for six (6) or
18 fewer children who are not the children, siblings, stepchildren, grandchildren,
19 nieces, nephews, or children in legal custody of the provider; and
20 (7) "Secretary" means the secretary of the cabinet.

22 TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
23 (1) Unless federal laws or regulations provide otherwise, applicants for the Child
24 Care Assistance Program operated by the cabinet utilizing federal funds under
25 the Child Care and Development Fund in accordance with 45 C.F.R. pt. 98,
26 whose occupation or employment in a specific industry sector has been
27 designated by the Cabinet for Economic Development as an in-demand
