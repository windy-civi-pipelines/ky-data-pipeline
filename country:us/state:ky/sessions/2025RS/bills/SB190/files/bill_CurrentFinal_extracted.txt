2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 238.540 (Effective July 1, 2025) is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) Charitable gaming shall be conducted by a licensed charitable organization at the
5 location, date, and time which shall be stated on the license. The licensee shall
6 request a change in the date, time, or location of a charitable gaming event by mail,
7 electronic mail, or facsimile transmission, and shall submit a lease and an original
8 signature of an officer. The office shall process this request and issue or deny a
9 license within ten (10) days.
10 (2) All premises or facilities on which or in which charitable gaming is conducted shall
11 meet all applicable federal, state, and local code requirements relating to life, safety,
12 and health.
13 (3) A license to conduct charitable gaming shall be prominently displayed on or in the
14 premises where charitable gaming is conducted, in a conspicuous location that is
15 readily accessible to gaming patrons as well as employees of the office, law
16 enforcement officials, and other interested officials.
17 (4) At least one (1) chairperson who is listed on the application for licensure shall be at
18 each charitable gaming activity conducted by the charitable organization and shall
19 be responsible for the administration and conduct of the charitable gaming activity.
20 A[No] person shall not serve as chairperson for more than one (1) charitable
21 organization. The chairperson shall be readily identifiable as the chairperson and
22 shall be present on the premises continuously during the charitable gaming activity.
23 Charitable gaming shall be conducted and administered solely by officers,
24 members, and bona fide employees of the licensed charitable organization.
25 Volunteer personnel, who may or may not be members of the licensed charitable
26 organization, may be utilized if each volunteer is readily identifiable as a volunteer.
27 A person may serve as volunteer personnel for up to six (6) charitable gaming

2 administration of charitable gaming shall not receive any compensation for services
3 related to the charitable gaming activities, including tipping.[ No] Net receipts
4 derived from charitable gaming shall not inure to the private benefit or financial
5 gain of any individual. Any effort or attempt to disguise any other type of
6 compensation or private inurement shall be considered an unauthorized diversion of

8 (5) A[No] licensed charitable organization shall not contract with, or otherwise utilize

3 Section 2. KRS 238.545 (Effective July 1, 2025) is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) A licensed charitable organization shall be limited by the following:
5 (a) In the conduct of bingo, to one (1) session per day, three (3)[two (2)] sessions
6 per week, for a period not to exceed five (5) consecutive hours in any day and
7 not to exceed fifteen (15)[ten (10)] total hours per week:
8 1. A[No] licensed charitable organization shall not conduct bingo at more
9 than one (1) location during the same twenty-four (24) hour period;
10 2. A[No] licensed charitable organization shall not award prizes for bingo
11 that exceed five thousand dollars ($5,000) in fair market value per
12 twenty-four (24) hour period, including the value of door prizes; and
13 3. A[No] person under the age of eighteen (18) shall not be permitted to
14 purchase bingo supplies or play bingo unless he or she is playing for
15 noncash prizes and is accompanied by a parent or legal guardian and
16 only if the value of any noncash prize awarded does not exceed ten
17 dollars ($10);
18 (b) 1. A licensed charitable organization may provide card-minding devices
19 for use by players of bingo games.
20 2. If a licensed charitable organization offers card-minding devices for use
21 by players, the devices shall be capable of being used in conjunction
22 with bingo cards or paper sheets at all times.
23 3. The office shall have broad authority to define and regulate the use of
24 card-minding devices and the corporation shall promulgate an
25 administrative regulation concerning use and control of them;
26 (c) Charity game tickets shall be sold only at the address of the location
27 designated on the license to conduct charitable gaming;
