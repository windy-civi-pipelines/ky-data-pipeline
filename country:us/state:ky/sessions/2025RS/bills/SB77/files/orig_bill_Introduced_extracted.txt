2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 161.028 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) The Education Professional Standards Board is recognized to be a public body
5 corporate and politic and an agency and instrumentality of the Commonwealth, in
6 the performance of essential governmental functions. The Education Professional
7 Standards Board has the authority and responsibility to:
8 (a) Establish standards and requirements for obtaining and maintaining a teaching
9 certificate;
10 (b) Set standards for, approve, and evaluate college, university, and school district
11 programs for the preparation of teachers and other professional school
12 personnel. College or university programs may be approved by the board for a
13 college or university with regional institutional level accreditation or national
14 institutional level accreditation that is recognized by the United States
15 Department of Education and is eligible to receive federal funding under 20
16 U.S.C. secs. 1061 to 1063. Program standards shall reflect national standards
17 and shall address, at a minimum, the following:
18 1. The alignment of programs with the state's core content for assessment
19 as defined in KRS 158.6457;
20 2. Research-based classroom practices, including effective classroom
21 management techniques;
22 3. Emphasis on subject matter competency of teacher education students;
23 4. Methodologies to meet diverse educational needs of all students;
24 5. The consistency and quality of classroom and field experiences,
25 including early practicums and student teaching experiences;
26 6. The amount of college-wide or university-wide involvement and support
27 during the preparation as well as the induction of new teachers;

1 members. The Governor shall make the following fifteen (15) appointments:
2 1. Nine (9) members who shall be teachers representative of elementary,
3 middle or junior high, secondary, special education, and secondary
4 vocational classrooms;
5 2. Two (2) members who shall be school administrators, one (1) of whom
6 shall be a school principal;
7 3. One (1) member representative of local boards of education; and
8 4. Three (3) members representative of postsecondary institutions, two (2)
9 of whom shall be deans of colleges of education at public universities
10 and one (1) of whom shall be the chief academic officer or designee of
11 an independent not-for-profit college or university.
12 (b) The members appointed by the Governor shall be confirmed by the Senate
13 under KRS 11.160. If the General Assembly is not in session at the time of the
14 appointment, persons appointed shall serve prior to confirmation, but the
15 Governor shall seek the consent of the Senate at the next regular session or at
16 an intervening extraordinary session if the matter is included in the call of the
17 General Assembly.
18 (c) Each appointed member shall serve a three (3) year term. A vacancy on the
19 board shall be filled in the same manner as the original appointment within
20 sixty (60) days after it occurs. A member shall continue to serve until his or
21 her successor is named. Any member who, through change of employment
22 status or residence, or for other reasons, no longer meets the criteria for the
23 position to which he or she was appointed shall no longer be eligible to serve
24 in that position.
25 (d) Members of the board shall serve without compensation but shall be permitted
26 to attend board meetings and perform other board business without loss of
27 income or other benefits.

2 district, required to hire a substitute for a member of the board who is absent

5 (f) A chairman shall be elected by and from the membership. A member shall be
6 eligible to serve no more than three (3) one (1) year terms in succession as
7 chairman. Regular meetings shall be held at least semiannually on call of the
8 chairman.
9 (g) The commissioner of education shall serve as executive secretary to the board
10 and may designate staff to facilitate his or her duties.
11 (h) To carry out the functions relating to its duties and responsibilities, the board
12 is empowered to receive donations and grants of funds; to appoint consultants
13 as needed; and to sponsor studies, conduct conferences, and publish
14 information.
