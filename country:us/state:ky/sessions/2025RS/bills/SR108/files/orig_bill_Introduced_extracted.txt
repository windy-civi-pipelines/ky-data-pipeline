1 WHEREAS, as we mark the 106th birthday of the Lexington-Fayette NAACP
2 branch, we honor the resilience, courage, and tireless efforts of those who have
3 contributed to this movement, past and present, as well as renew our commitment to
4 carrying forward their vision of a world in which all people can live with dignity and
5 without fear of discrimination; and
6 WHEREAS, the Lexington-Fayette NAACP branch continues to stand resolutely
7 against systemic racism and injustice, advocating for transformative change in laws,
8 policies, and institutions to create a future of equal opportunity for all, regardless of race
9 or background; and
10 WHEREAS, on this special day, we reflect on the ongoing need for the work of the
11 Lexington-Fayette NAACP branch, recognizing both the progress made and the
12 challenges that remain in achieving true racial equality and justice in Lexington and
13 across this great Commonwealth;
14 NOW, THEREFORE,
15 Be it resolved by the Senate of the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of
16 Kentucky:
17 Section 1. The members of the Senate, both individually and collectively,
18 commemorate the 106th anniversary of the Lexington-Fayette NAACP branch and honor
19 the vision, perseverance, and leadership of those who laid its foundation to advance the
20 cause of civil rights for all people of the Commonwealth and beyond.
21 Section 2. The Clerk of the Senate is directed to transmit a copy of this
22 Resolution to Senator Reginald Thomas for delivery.
