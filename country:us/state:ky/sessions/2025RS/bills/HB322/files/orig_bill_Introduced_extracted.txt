UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 487
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 164.2841 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) (a) Any person whose parent or any nonmarried widow or widower whose spouse
5 was a resident of the Commonwealth of Kentucky upon becoming a law
6 enforcement officer, firefighter, [or ]volunteer firefighter, or emergency
7 medical services personnel and who was killed while in active service or
8 training for active service or who died as a result of a service-connected
9 disability shall not be required to pay any matriculation or tuition fee upon
10 admission to any state-supported university, community college, or vocational
11 training institution. The provisions of this subsection shall apply to any
12 firefighter or volunteer firefighter who is killed or dies under the conditions
13 covered in this subsection on July 1, 1989, or thereafter. The provisions of
14 this subsection shall apply to any emergency medical services personnel
15 who was killed or died under the conditions covered in this subsection on
16 July 1, 2000, or thereafter.
17 (b) In order to obtain the benefits conferred by paragraph (a) of this subsection,
18 the parent-child relationship shall be shown by birth certificate, adoption
19 papers, or other documentary evidence. The spousal relationship shall be
20 shown by a marriage certificate or other documentary evidence. The parent's
21 or spouse's service and the cause of death shall be evidenced by certification
22 from the records of the Kentucky Justice and Public Safety Cabinet, the
23 appropriate city or county law enforcement agency which employed the
24 deceased, the administrative agency for the fire department or fire protection
25 district recognized for funding under KRS 95A.262, [or ]the administrative
26 agency having jurisdiction over any paid firefighters of all counties and cities
27 of all classes, or the county, city, or administrative agency employing the

1 emergency medical services personnel.
2 (2) (a) Any person whose parent or any nonmarried widow or widower whose spouse
3 was an employee participating in a state-administered retirement system, and
4 not otherwise covered by subsection (1) of this section, and who died as a
5 result of a duty-related injury as described in KRS 61.621 shall not be
6 required to pay any matriculation or tuition fee upon admission to any state-
7 supported university, community college, or vocational training institution.
8 (b) In order to obtain the benefits conferred by paragraph (a) of this subsection,
9 the parent-child relationship shall be shown by birth certificate, adoption
10 papers, or other documentary evidence. The spousal relationship shall be
11 shown by a marriage certificate or other documentary evidence. The parent's
12 or spouse's service and the cause of death shall be evidenced by certification
13 from the records of the employing agency or the appropriate retirement
14 system.
15 (3) If one so admitted to a state-supported university, community college, or vocational
16 training institution under the provisions of this section shall have obtained a cash
17 scholarship paid or payable to the institution, from whatever source, the amount of
18 the scholarship shall be applied to the credit of the applicant in the payment of
19 incidental expenses of his attendance at the institution, and any balance, if the terms
20 of the scholarship permit, shall be returned to the applicant.
21 (4) As used in this section, "emergency medical services personnel" has the same
22 meaning as in KRS 61.315.
