Section 1:
UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 120
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 158.165 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) (a) The board of education of each school district shall adopt[develop] a policy
5 regarding the possession and use of a personal telecommunications device by
6 a student while on school property or while attending a school-sponsored or
7 school-related activity on or off school property, and shall include the policy
8 in the district's written standards of student conduct.
9 (b) The policy shall, at a minimum, forbid a student's use of a personal
10 telecommunications device during the school day, except during an
11 emergency or if directed to do so by a teacher for an instructional purpose.
12 (c) A student who violates the policy shall be subject to discipline as provided by
13 board policy.
14 (2) As used in this section, "personal telecommunications device":
15 (a) Means a device that emits an audible signal, vibrates, displays a message, or
16 otherwise summons or delivers a communication to the possessor, including[,]
17 but not limited to[,] a paging device and a cellular telephone; and
18 (b) Does not include any device a student is authorized to use pursuant to the
19 Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, 20 U.S.C. sec. 1400 et seq., the
20 Americans with Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. sec. 12101 et seq., or the
21 Rehabilitation Act of 1973, 29 U.S.C. sec. 701 et seq., or successor acts.
22 Section 2. KRS 156.675 is amended to read as follows:
23 (1) The Kentucky Board of Education shall promulgate administrative regulations to
24 prevent social media and sexually explicit material from being transmitted via any
25 video or computer system, software or hardware product, or Internet service
26 managed or provided to local schools or school districts.
27 (2) Each local school district and school shall utilize the latest available filtering

3 (3) The Kentucky Department of Education shall make available to school districts and

5 local districts and schools to prevent access to social media and sexually explicit

8 school shall meet the requirements of subsection (2) of this section.
9 (4) Each local school district shall establish a policy regarding student internet access
10 that shall include[,] but not be limited to[,] parental consent for student internet use,
11 teacher supervision of student computer use, and auditing procedures to determine
12 whether education technology is being used for the purpose of accessing social
13 media or sexually explicit or other objectionable material.
14 (5) The provisions of subsections (1) to (4) of this section shall only apply to social
15 media that a student is not authorized by a teacher to access for an instructional
16 purpose.
