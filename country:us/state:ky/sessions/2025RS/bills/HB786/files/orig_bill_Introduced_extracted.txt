UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 1894
2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS 311.530 TO 311.620 IS CREATED
4 TO READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) Notwithstanding any of the requirements for medical licensure in KRS 311.571,
6 an international medical graduate may apply for a provisional license that may
7 lead to a full and unrestricted license to practice medicine in Kentucky.
8 (2) By January 1, 2026, the board shall promulgate administrative regulations in
9 accordance with KRS Chapter 13A that establish a provisional licensure pathway
10 for international medical graduates to obtain a full and unrestricted license to
11 practice medicine in Kentucky. The administrative regulations shall:
12 (a) Establish criteria for acceptable foreign medical schools that provide
13 medical education that is reasonably comparable to those of medical
14 schools in the United States from which applicants may be accepted;
15 (b) Require that the applicant have an offer of full-time employment as a
16 physician from a Kentucky health care provider and:
17 1. Maintain employment for at least two (2) consecutive years; and
18 2. Notify the board within five (5) business days of any change of
19 employer;
20 (c) Require that the applicant has:
21 1. An unencumbered license in a foreign country;
22 2. A license in good standing for the immediately preceding five (5)
23 years, without pending disciplinary action;
24 3. Actively practiced medicine for the entire five (5) year period
25 preceding his or her application;
26 4. Completed a residency or substantially similar postgraduate medical
27 residency program accredited by the Accreditation Council for

3 Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates; and
4 6. Passed the Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates
5 examinations, including the United States Medical Licensing
6 Examination Steps 1 and 2 and the Occupational English Test,
7 Medicine;
8 (d) Establish eligibility requirements for a full and unrestricted license to
9 practice medicine in Kentucky upon successfully completing a provisional
10 licensure period, including:
11 1. Board assessment of each applicant on his or her merits;
12 2. A requirement that the applicant not have any disciplinary actions or
13 investigations pending from his or her provisional licensure period;
14 3. Evidence of fulfillment of employment requirements during the
15 provisional period; and
16 4. No evidence of engaging in unacceptable conduct under KRS 311.595
17 or 311.597; and
18 (e) 1. Establish a process to collect information to facilitate evaluation of the
19 provisional license pathway to determine whether it is meeting the
20 intended purpose. The information shall include but not be limited to:
21 a. The number of applicants;
22 b. The number of internationally trained physicians receiving
23 provisional licensure under the pathway and the number denied
24 provisional licensure under the pathway;
25 c. The number of individuals achieving full and unrestricted
26 licensure;
