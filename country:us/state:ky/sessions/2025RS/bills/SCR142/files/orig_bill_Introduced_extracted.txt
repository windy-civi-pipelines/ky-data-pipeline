4 WHEREAS, artificial intelligence has the potential to revolutionize industries and
5 immensely improve the lives of Kentuckians; and
6 WHEREAS, artificial intelligence has the power to drive economic growth,
7 enhance efficiency, and personalize services; and

1 Kentucky, the House of Representatives concurring therein:
2 Section 1. The Legislative Research Commission shall establish the
3 Commonwealth Artificial Intelligence Consortium Task Force to serve as a collaborative
4 platform to bring together state and local governments, educational institutions,
5 healthcare providers, industry, and citizens to identify needs, collect data, develop
6 artificial intelligence solutions, foster innovation and competitiveness, promote artificial
7 intelligence literacy, and ensure trusted artificial intelligence development and
8 governance.
9 Section 2. The task force shall be composed of the following members with
10 final membership of the task force being subject to the consideration and approval of the
11 Legislative Research Commission:
12 (1) Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of
13 the House of Representatives, one of whom shall be designated as co-chair;
14 (2) One member of the House of Representatives appointed by the Minority Floor
15 Leader of the House of Representatives;
16 (3) Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, one of
17 whom shall be designated as co-chair;
18 (4) One member of the Senate appointed by the Minority Floor Leader of the
19 Senate;
20 (5) The president of the Council on Postsecondary Education or designee;
21 (6) The executive director of the Kentucky League of Cities or designee;
22 (7) The executive director of the Kentucky Association of Counties or designee;
23 (8) The deputy chief information officer of the Commonwealth Office of
24 Technology or designee;
25 (9) The secretary of the Cabinet for Health and Family Services or designee;
26 (10) Two representatives from the Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
27 Hub at the University of Kentucky; and

UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 2001
1 (11) Two representatives from the J.B. Speed School of Engineering at the
2 University of Louisville.
3 Section 3. The task force shall meet as needed during the 2025 Interim of the
4 General Assembly. The task force shall submit its findings and legislative
5 recommendations to the Legislative Research Commission by November 21, 2025. If
6 legislative recommendations are submitted, the Legislative Research Commission may
7 refer the recommendations to the appropriate committee or committees of jurisdiction in
8 advance of the 2026 Regular Session of the Kentucky General Assembly.
9 Section 4. Provisions of this Resolution to the contrary notwithstanding, the
10 Legislative Research Commission shall have the authority to alternatively assign the
11 issues identified herein to an interim joint committee or subcommittee thereof and to
12 designate a study completion date.
