4 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 164 IS CREATED TO
5 READ AS FOLLOWS:
6 (1) The Kentucky Nuclear Energy Development Authority established in Section 2 of
7 this Act shall create and implement the Nuclear Energy Development Grant
8 Program for the advancement and location of nuclear energy-related projects to
9 support the entire nuclear energy ecosystem in the Commonwealth, including
10 utility and private sector economic development activities. The nuclear energy
11 ecosystem includes but is not limited to:
12 (a) The nuclear fuel cycle, which includes fuel conversion, enrichment, and
13 fabrication, as well as potential future spent fuel recycling and
14 reprocessing;
15 (b) Reactor design and component manufacturing;
16 (c) Component supply chain manufacturing and distribution;
17 (d) Facility siting and development;
18 (e) Radioisotope production;
19 (f) Facility operation and maintenance;
20 (g) Decommissioning waste storage, transport, and management; and
21 (h) End uses of nuclear energy and co-products.
22 (2) The membership of the Kentucky Nuclear Energy Development Authority shall
23 select five (5) of its voting members to serve on the nuclear energy development
24 grant administration subcommittee. A majority of the members of the grant
25 administration subcommittee shall constitute a quorum for the purposes of doing
26 business. The subcommittee shall:
27 (a) Create grant applications;

1 (b) Establish grant applicant eligibility requirements;
2 (c) Establish objective scoring criteria to evaluate grant applications, including
3 but not limited to:
4 1. The likelihood that the proposed project funded by the grant will
5 precipitate further investment in the nuclear energy ecosystem;
6 2. The economic impact of the grant funding on the community and
7 region where the proposed project will be located;
8 3. The readiness of the community where the proposed project will be
9 located to host nuclear-related investments, including whether the
10 community has been awarded a nuclear-ready community designation
11 under KRS 164.2804; and
12 4. The amount of additional investment that would be made in the
13 proposed project by the grant applicant and other sources; and
14 (d) Make determinations on grant eligibility and funding and make grant
15 awards based on those determinations, not to exceed two million dollars
16 ($2,000,000) per individual grant.
