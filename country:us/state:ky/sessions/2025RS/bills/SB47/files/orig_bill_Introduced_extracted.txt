2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 SECTION 1. A NEW SECTION OF KRS CHAPTER 141 IS CREATED TO
4 READ AS FOLLOWS:
5 (1) As used in this section, "qualifying child" has the same meaning as in Section
6 152 of the Internal Revenue Code, but only includes individuals under the age of
7 six (6) at the end of the taxable year for purposes of the Kentucky child credit
8 permitted by this section.
9 (2) For taxable years beginning on or after January 1, 2026, but before January 1,
10 2030, taxpayers may claim a refundable child credit against the tax imposed
11 under KRS 141.020, with the ordering of the credit as provided in Section 2 of
12 this Act.
13 (3) Part-year resident taxpayers shall be allowed a prorated Kentucky child credit
14 based upon their portion of Kentucky adjusted gross income as determined by
15 KRS 141.019 to their adjusted gross income as defined in Section 62 of the
16 Internal Revenue Code.
17 (4) (a) The maximum amount of the credit that may be claimed under this section
18 shall be determined under paragraphs (b) and (c) of this subsection.
19 (b) Individuals with modified gross incomes of less than:
20 1. Fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) for single taxpayers or taxpayers
21 filing a separate return; or
22 2. One hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) for married taxpayers filing
23 separately on a combined form or a joint tax return;
24 shall be eligible for a credit of one thousand dollars ($1,000) per qualifying
25 child.
26 (c) Individuals with modified gross incomes in excess of the thresholds set forth
27 in paragraph (b) of this subsection may be eligible for a credit of one

1 thousand dollars ($1,000) per qualifying child reduced by ten dollars ($10)
2 for every one dollar ($1) above the income thresholds.
3 (5) In order for the General Assembly to evaluate the effectiveness of this credit, the
4 department shall report the following information to the Interim Joint Committee
5 on Appropriations and Revenue on or before November 1, 2027, and on or before
6 each November 1 thereafter, as long as the Kentucky child credit is claimed:
7 (a) The location of the taxpayer, by county, as reflected on the return filed for
8 the taxable year;
9 (b) The amount of the Kentucky child credit claimed by the taxpayer for the
10 taxable year;
11 (c) The total cumulative amount of all credits claimed for the taxable year; and
12 (d) Based on ranges of adjusted gross income of no larger than five thousand
13 dollars ($5,000) for the taxable year, the total amount of credits claimed and
14 the number of returns claiming a credit for each adjusted gross income
15 range.
16 (6) The information required to be reported under this section shall not be
17 considered confidential taxpayer information and shall not be subject to KRS
18 Chapter 131 or any other provisions of the Kentucky Revised Statutes prohibiting
19 disclosure or reporting of information.
