3 WHEREAS, Albert Robinson was born on December 19, 1938, at Silver Mine

5 WHEREAS, Albert Robinson spent his childhood in the East Bernstadt area of

7 WHEREAS, Albert Robinson earned a degree from Cumberland College, now

9 WHEREAS, he loved farming, fishing, and hunting. His farm was his earthly
10 paradise, which he spent his lifetime improving; and
11 WHEREAS, he greatly loved God, his family, friends, state, and country. His
12 commitment to Christ was the driving force in his decision-making, both in his public
13 and private life. Senate President Robert Stivers remarked that "his unwavering

15 knew and worked with him."; and

17 Commonwealth spanned over 50 years, as a member of the Kentucky House of
18 Representatives from 1972 to 1985 and 1987 to 1989, and as a member of the Kentucky

20 in the Senate, he served on the Banking and Insurance, State and Local Government,

22 served as chair of the Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee from
23 2015 to 2021; and
24 WHEREAS, throughout his life, Albert Robinson was a natural-born leader and
25 entrepreneur who was selflessly devoted to both his community and this Commonwealth.

1 and entrepreneur that did big things without seeking glory for himself."; and
2 WHEREAS, his calling to serve the citizens of his community was quickly
3 recognized by prominent leaders of this Commonwealth. In the words of Attorney

7 remarked on how Senator Albert Robinson was a "selfless public servant" who
8 "dedicated nearly half a century to serving our Commonwealth, holding positions in both

18 Kentucky:

20 the passing of Senator Albert Robinson, and posthumously thanks him for his service to
21 the Commonwealth.
22 Section 2. When the Senate adjourns this day, it does so in honor and loving
23 memory of Senator Albert Robinson.
24 Section 3. The Clerk of the Senate is directed to transmit a copy of this
25 Resolution to Senator Brandon Storm for delivery.
