UNOFFICIAL COPY 25 RS BR 1325
1 A RESOLUTION recognizing January 27, 2025, as International Holocaust
2 Remembrance Day.
3 WHEREAS, the Holocaust was the state-sponsored, systematic persecution and
4 attempted annihilation of European Jewry by Nazi Germany and its collaborators
5 between 1933 and 1945 during which six million Jews were murdered; and
6 WHEREAS, the history of the Holocaust offers an opportunity to reflect on the
7 moral responsibilities of individuals, societies, and governments; and
8 WHEREAS, we the people of the Commonwealth of Kentucky should always
9 remember the terrible events of the Holocaust and remain vigilant against hatred,
10 persecution, and tyranny; and
11 WHEREAS, in the aftermath of the October 7, 2023, atrocities, the bloodiest day in
12 Jewish history since the Holocaust, we are reminded of the vow "Never Again"; and
13 WHEREAS, rising antisemitism in the United States makes clear the need for
14 continued vigilance and dedication to the principles of individual freedom in a just
15 society; and
16 WHEREAS, the Kentucky Jewish Council's annual report on antisemitism makes
17 clear our need to combat antisemitism here in the Commonwealth; and
18 WHEREAS, on November 1, 2005, the United Nations General Assembly adopted
19 Resolution 60/7 to designate January 27 as International Holocaust Remembrance Day to
20 correspond with the anniversary of the liberation of Auschwitz-Birkenau, and it is meant
21 to honor the victims of Nazism and to condemn all forms of "religious intolerance,
22 incitement, harassment or violence against persons or communities based on ethnic origin
23 or religious belief" throughout the world;
24 NOW, THEREFORE,
25 Be it resolved by the Senate of the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of
26 Kentucky:
27 Section 1. The members of the Senate hereby recognize January 27, 2025, as
