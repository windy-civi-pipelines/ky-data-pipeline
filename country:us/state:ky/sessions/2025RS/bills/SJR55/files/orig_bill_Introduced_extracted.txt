3 WHEREAS, Jewish students are targeted for hate crimes at a higher per capita rate

10 antisemitism and encouraging public officials, including the presidents of our public

19 Be it resolved by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
20 Section 1. By January 1, 2026, the governing board of each of Kentucky's
21 public postsecondary education institutions shall adopt policies to combat antisemitism
22 within their institutions. The institutions shall implement these policies through June 30,
23 2028, and may continue implementation thereafter. Policies shall require:
24 (1) At the start of each semester, notifying all students:
25 (a) Of the rights that are protected at the institution under Title VI of the Civil
26 Rights Act of 1964 and how a student may file a complaint if the student believes those
27 rights have been violated; and

17 1964 that are connected to antisemitism of which the institution has been notified and the
18 results of those investigations that are in the possession of the institution; and
19 (c) The number of actions that have been brought against the institution under
20 KRS 344.450 and the outcomes of such actions.
