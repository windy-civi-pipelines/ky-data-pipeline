2 Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Kentucky:
3 Section 1. KRS 164.787 is amended to read as follows:
4 (1) The General Assembly hereby establishes the Work Ready Kentucky Scholarship
5 Program to ensure that all Kentuckians who have not yet earned a postsecondary
6 degree have affordable access to an industry-recognized certificate, diploma, or
7 associate of applied science degree and, for students with intellectual disabilities
8 enrolled in comprehensive transition and postsecondary programs, affordable
9 access to meaningful credentials to prepare for competitive integrated employment.
10 (2) For purposes of this section:
11 (a) "Academic term" means a fall, spring, or summer academic term or other time
12 period specified in an administrative regulation promulgated by the authority;
13 (b) "Academic year" means July 1 through June 30 of each year;
14 (c) "Adjusted gross income" means the federal adjusted gross income reported
15 on an eligible workforce student's Free Application for Federal Student Aid
16 for the academic year in which the scholarship is awarded;
17 (d) "Approved dual credit course" means a dual credit course developed in
18 accordance with KRS 164.098 that is a career and technical education course
19 within a career pathway approved by the Kentucky Department of Education
20 that leads to an industry-recognized credential;
21 (e)[(d)] "Dual credit tuition rate ceiling" means the same as defined in 164.786;
22 (f)[(e)] "Eligible institution" means an institution defined in KRS 164.001 that:
23 1. Actively participates in the federal Pell Grant program;
24 2. Executes a contract with the authority on terms the authority deems
25 necessary or appropriate for the administration of its programs;
26 3. Charges no more than the dual credit tuition rate ceiling per credit hour,
27 including any additional fees, for any dual credit course it offers to any

6 accredited by a recognized regional or national accrediting body
7 and licensed to operate at a site in Kentucky;

4 (j)[(i)] Following the first academic term scholarship funds are received,
5 achieve and maintain satisfactory academic progress as determined by the

11 (6) (a) Beginning with the 2019-2020 academic year, the authority shall award a
12 Work Ready Kentucky Scholarship each academic term to any person who
13 meets the requirements of this section to the extent funds are available for that
14 purpose.
15 (b) The scholarship amount awarded to an eligible workforce student for an
16 academic term shall be the amount remaining after subtracting the student's
17 federal and state grants and scholarships from the maximum scholarship
18 amount. The maximum scholarship amount shall be the per credit hour in-
19 state tuition rate at the Kentucky Community and Technical College System
20 multiplied by the number of credit hours in which the student is enrolled and
21 the fees charged to the student. The authority shall promulgate an
22 administrative regulation in accordance with KRS Chapter 13A to specify the
23 maximum amount to be awarded for fees, except that for the 2019-2020
24 academic year the amount awarded for fees shall not exceed four hundred
25 dollars ($400).
26 (c) The scholarship award for an eligible high school student shall be limited to
27 two (2) approved dual credit courses per academic year. The scholarship

5 student's eligibility for the scholarship shall terminate upon the earlier of:
6 1. Receiving the scholarship for a total of sixty (60) credit hours; or
7 2. Obtaining an associate's degree.
8 (b) For an eligible workforce student enrolled in a comprehensive transition and
9 postsecondary program, eligibility for the scholarship shall terminate upon the
10 earlier of completing the program or receiving the scholarship for up to nine
11 (9) academic terms within three (3) academic years.
12 (8) The authority shall annually provide a report on the Work Ready Kentucky
13 Scholarship Program, prepared in collaboration with the Office for Education and
14 Workforce Statistics, to the secretary of the Education and Labor Cabinet that
15 includes, by academic term, academic year, institution, and workforce sector, the
16 number of:
17 (a) Students served by the scholarship and the total amount disbursed;
18 (b) Credits, certificates, diplomas, and associate of applied science degrees earned
19 by students receiving the scholarship; and
20 (c) Students receiving the scholarship who are enrolled in a comprehensive
21 transition and postsecondary program and credentials earned by those
22 students.
23 (9) The authority shall report Work Ready Kentucky Scholarship program data to the
24 Office for Education and Workforce Statistics for analysis of the program's success
25 in meeting the goal of increasing skilled workforce participation rates.

5 section.
6 (c) Any unallotted or unencumbered balances in the trust fund shall be invested
7 as provided in KRS 42.500(9). Income earned from the investments shall be
8 credited to the trust fund.
9 (d) Notwithstanding KRS 45.229, any fund balance at the close of the fiscal year
10 shall not lapse but shall be carried forward to the next fiscal year and
11 continuously appropriated only for the purposes specified in this section.
